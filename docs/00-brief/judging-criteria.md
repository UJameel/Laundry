# Judging Criteria

## Official Rubric (100 points total)

### 1. Real-World Impact — 25 pts (HIGHEST)
**What judges care about:** Does this solve a genuine problem that real people in finance have? Is it more than a tech demo? Could this actually get adopted?
- Pick a specific, painful workflow — not a vague "AI for finance" concept
- Show you understand the user and their pain
- Bonus: if you can cite numbers (time saved, error reduction, cost impact)

### 2. Product Execution & Demo Quality — 25 pts (TIED HIGHEST)
**What judges care about:** Does it work? Is it polished? Does the demo tell a clear story?
- Working product > slideware. Always.
- Demo must flow smoothly in 3 minutes — no fumbling
- UI matters: judges form opinions in the first 10 seconds of seeing your screen
- Use Lovable for rapid UI — the whole point of the sponsor credits

### 3. Problem Relevance & Clarity — 20 pts
**What judges care about:** Is this a real problem? Can you articulate it clearly and concisely?
- Open with the problem, not the solution
- 1 sentence: who has this problem and why it hurts
- Don't oversell — credibility matters with this judge panel

### 4. Use of AI / Technical Creativity — 15 pts
**What judges care about:** Is the AI usage meaningful or bolted on? Is there something clever in the approach?
- AI should be core to the solution, not a gimmick
- Using multiple modalities (text + audio via MiniMax) can differentiate
- n8n workflows show technical architecture thinking
- Don't over-engineer — clever simplicity > complex mess

### 5. Feasibility & Security — 15 pts
**What judges care about:** Could this actually be built and deployed? Is it secure enough for finance?
- Finance = regulated. Even a prototype should acknowledge security basics
- Show awareness of data sensitivity (don't casually log PII in your demo)
- Architecture should be plausible beyond the hackathon
- Crossmint for payments shows you're thinking about real infrastructure

## Inferred / Unstated Criteria

- **Sponsor tool integration:** 4 sponsors with credits = using their tools well will be noticed, especially by HAC organizers. Aim for 2-3 sponsors meaningfully integrated.
- **Storytelling:** With judges from product (Intuit), GTM (Google), and banking (Mercury, SBI), narrative quality matters as much as code quality.
- **Finance domain authenticity:** This isn't a generic hack. Judges will know if your "finance problem" is made up. Pick something real.
- **Team energy / presentation:** In-person events — your enthusiasm and clarity during the live demo are weighted informally.

## Scoring Strategy

With only 100 points and this distribution:
- **50% of score = Impact + Demo quality.** This is where you win or lose. A polished demo of a real problem beats a technically brilliant but poorly presented prototype.
- **20% = Problem clarity.** Nail the opening of your pitch. One slide, one sentence.
- **30% = Tech + Feasibility.** Important but not the differentiator. Don't sacrifice demo time for technical depth.

**Time allocation implication:** Spend 60% of time on building something demoable, 25% on demo prep, 15% on technical depth.
